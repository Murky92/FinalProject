Getting Started

Welcome to Tabletop Reserve! This guide will walk you through everything you need to know to make the most of our platform for your gaming shop.

Registration Process

To begin using Tabletop Reserve, you'll need to complete the shop registration process:

  1. Click the "Register Shop" button in the top navigation
  2. Complete the registration form with your shop details
  3. Submit your application for review
  4. Receive approval email (usually within 1-2 business days)
  5. Login to access your shop dashboard

Note: Your shop will need to be approved by our team before it appears in the player app. Make sure to provide accurate information to speed up the approval process.

Dashboard Overview

Once approved, you'll have access to your shop dashboard, which includes:

  • Dashboard Home: See an overview of your shop's performance
  • Shop Profile: Manage your shop's basic information
  • Tables: Set up and manage your gaming tables
  • Schedule: Define your opening hours
  • Events: Create and manage special gaming events
  • Reservations: View and manage table bookings
  • Notifications: Communicate with your customers

The shop owner dashboard gives you quick access to all features

Setting Up Your Profile

Your shop profile is your digital storefront in the Tabletop Reserve app. A complete, attractive profile significantly increases your chances of attracting players.

Basic Information

Start by filling out your basic shop information:

  • Shop Name: Your official business name
  • Owner Name: Your name or the primary contact
  • Description: A compelling description of your shop (250-300 words recommended)
  • Shop Type: Select the category that best fits your establishment

Logo and Images

Visual elements are crucial for attracting customers:

  • Upload a high-quality logo (recommended size: 500x500px or larger)
  • Add at least 5-10 photos of your shop, focusing on gaming spaces
  • Include images of special features (terrain, premium tables, etc.)
  • Consider adding photos from events to showcase your community
üí°
Pro Tip: High-quality photos significantly increase booking rates. Consider hiring a professional photographer or using a smartphone with good lighting.

Contact Information

Ensure your contact information is accurate and up-to-date:

  • Email: The primary contact email (will receive booking notifications)
  • Phone: Your shop's phone number
  • Address: Complete physical address with postal code
  • Website: Your shop's website (if applicable)
  • Social Media: Links to your Facebook, Instagram, Twitter, etc.

Shop Details

Add specific details about your shop to help customers find what they're looking for:

  • Amenities: Wi-Fi, food, drinks, parking, accessibility features
  • Specialties: Particular games or genres you focus on
  • Payment Methods: What payment types you accept
  • House Rules: Any specific rules players should know

Managing Tables

Setting up your tables correctly is essential for managing bookings effectively. Each table represents a bookable space in your shop.

Adding Tables

To add a new table to your inventory:

  1. Go to the "Tables" section in your dashboard
  2. Click "Add New Table"
  3. Fill out the required information
  4. Save the table

The table creation form with sample data

Table Information

When setting up a table, include these details:

  • Table Name/Number: A unique identifier (e.g., "Table 1" or "Red Dragon Table")
  • Capacity: Maximum number of players the table can accommodate
  • Table Type: Standard, Large, RPG, Miniatures, Card Game, etc.
  • Description: Details about the table's features and best uses
  • Specialized Game: If the table is set up for a specific game
  • Features: Special amenities like terrain, power outlets, lighting
  • Hourly Rate: Cost per hour (if applicable)
  • Minimum Booking: Minimum time required for a booking

Table Visibility

Control when and how your tables appear to customers:

  • Use the "Active" toggle to show/hide tables from search results
  • Temporarily deactivate tables that are undergoing maintenance
  • Set blackout dates for tables that aren't available during specific events
üí°
Pro Tip: Create different table types with varied amenities and price points to cater to different customer needs and maximize your space usage.

Managing Multiple Tables

If you have many tables to manage:

  • Use the bulk edit feature to update multiple tables at once
  • Create table templates for faster setup of similar tables
  • Use table categories to organize your inventory (e.g., RPG tables, wargaming tables)
  • Consider using table zones if your shop has distinct areas

Setting Your Schedule

Your shop schedule determines when customers can book tables. Setting accurate hours is crucial for managing expectations.

Regular Opening Hours

To set your regular opening hours:

  1. Go to the "Schedule" section in your dashboard
  2. For each day of the week, toggle whether you're open or closed
  3. Set opening and closing times for each day you're open
  4. Save your changes

The schedule interface showing weekly opening hours

Special Hours and Closures

For holidays, special events, or temporary changes:

  • Use the "Special Hours" feature to set one-time schedule changes
  • Add holiday closures in advance
  • Set extended hours for special events
‚ÑπÔ∏è
Note: Customers will be notified automatically if your hours change and they have an existing reservation.

Booking Windows Coming Soon

Control when and how far in advance customers can make reservations:

  • Minimum Notice: How far in advance bookings must be made (e.g., 2 hours)
  • Maximum Advance: How far in the future customers can book (e.g., 30 days)
  • Booking Intervals: Time slots available for booking (e.g., hourly, half-hourly)

Time Slot Management Coming Soon

Optimize your schedule with these strategies:

  • Consider staggering reservation start times to manage customer flow
  • Add buffer time between bookings for cleaning if needed
  • Create peak and off-peak pricing by adjusting hourly rates for different times

Creating Events

Events are a powerful way to attract new customers and build community around your shop. Tabletop Reserve makes it easy to create, promote, and manage gaming events.

Adding a New Event

To create a new event:

  1. Go to the "Events" section in your dashboard
  2. Click "Add New Event"
  3. Fill out the event details
  4. Set recurrence options if applicable
  5. Save and publish the event

The event creation form with example data

Event Information

Include these key details for each event:

  • Event Name: Clear, descriptive title
  • Date and Time: When the event takes place
  • Duration: How long the event will last
  • Description: Detailed information about the event
  • Game System: What game will be played
  • Experience Level: Beginner-friendly, all levels, experienced players only
  • Attendance Limit: Maximum number of participants
  • Registration Fee: Cost to participate (if any)
  • Prize Information: Details about prizes or tournaments

Recurring Events

For regular events like weekly game nights or monthly tournaments:

  • Enable the "Recurring Event" option
  • Select frequency (daily, weekly, monthly)
  • Choose specific days for weekly events
  • Set an end date or number of occurrences
üí°
Pro Tip: Regular events build community! Shops with weekly recurring events see up to 40% higher customer retention rates.

Event Promotion Coming Soon

Maximize attendance with these promotion strategies:

  • Use the "Send Notification" feature to alert customers about new events
  • Add eye-catching event images
  • Highlight special guests, prizes, or unique aspects
  • Schedule events at least 2 weeks in advance for better visibility
  • Share the event link on your social media channels

Managing Event Registrations

Track and manage event participants:

  • View the list of registered participants in the event dashboard
  • Send updates or changes to all registered players
  • Enable waitlists for popular events
  • Process check-ins on the day of the event

Managing Reservations

The reservations system is at the core of Tabletop Reserve. Learning to manage bookings effectively will help you provide a smooth experience for your customers.

Viewing Reservations

Access your reservations in two different views:

  • List View: Chronological list of all reservations
  • Calendar View: Visual calendar showing bookings across all tables

The calendar view provides a visual overview of all bookings

Reservation Statuses

Understand the different reservation statuses:

  • Pending: New reservations awaiting confirmation
  • Confirmed: Reservations you've approved
  • Completed: Past reservations that were fulfilled
  • Cancelled: Reservations that were cancelled by you or the customer

Manual Reservations

Create reservations on behalf of customers who call or visit:

  1. Go to the "Reservations" section
  2. Click "Add Reservation"
  3. Fill in the customer and reservation details
  4. Select "Confirmed" status to bypass the confirmation step
  5. Save the reservation
‚ÑπÔ∏è
Note: Consider collecting a deposit for manual reservations, especially during peak times or for large groups.

Managing Changes and Cancellations

Handle reservation modifications:

  • Edit any reservation details by clicking the "Edit" button
  • Process cancellations with the "Cancel" button
  • Provide a reason when cancelling to help with customer communication
  • Check table availability before changing dates or times

Cancellation Policy Coming Soon

Set a clear cancellation policy in your shop settings:

  • Define how much notice is required for cancellations
  • Set different policies for different table types if needed
  • Determine refund amounts for late cancellations
  • Consider no-show policies to protect your business
üí°
Pro Tip: Send a confirmation message to customers after each booking to reduce no-shows. Shops with automated confirmations see up to 30% fewer missed appointments.

Customer Communication

Effective communication with your customers is key to building loyalty and increasing return visits. Tabletop Reserve provides several tools to stay connected.

Notification Types

You can send three types of notifications to customers:

  • Shop Updates: General information about your shop
  • Promotions: Special offers, discounts, or deals
  • Event Announcements: Information about upcoming events

The notification creation interface

Sending Notifications

To send a notification to your customers:

  1. Go to the "Notifications" section
  2. Click "Send Notification"
  3. Select the notification type
  4. Write a clear title and message
  5. Choose target recipients
  6. Review and send

Target Audiences

Send messages to specific customer segments:

  • All Customers: Everyone who has the app and follows your shop
  • Followers Only: Customers who have favorited your shop
  • Recent Customers: People who have visited in the last 30 days
  • Event Participants: People registered for a specific event
‚ÑπÔ∏è
Note: To comply with communication regulations, customers can opt out of promotional messages while still receiving essential booking notifications.

Notification Best Practices

Maximize the effectiveness of your communications:

  • Keep messages concise (under 200 characters for best results)
  • Use clear, action-oriented language
  • Limit promotional notifications to 1-2 per week
  • Time promotions strategically (e.g., weekend announcements on Thursday)
  • Include essential information (what, when, where)
  • Add value with each notification (avoid "noise")
üí°
Pro Tip: The most effective notifications promote time-limited offers or events with clear value propositions. Try "Friday Night Magic: 20% off entry this week only!" rather than "We have Magic events."

Payments & Billing Coming Soon

Understanding how payments work on Tabletop Reserve will help you manage your business finances effectively.

Note: Our payment processing system is currently under development and will be available in a future release. Below is an overview of the planned functionality.

Payment Setup

To receive payments through the platform:

  1. Go to "Payment Settings" in your shop dashboard
  2. Connect your bank account
  3. Provide your tax information
  4. Set up your payout method and frequency

Commission Structure

Tabletop Reserve operates on a simple fee structure:

  • 5% commission on standard reservations
  • 3% commission for premium account holders
  • 7% commission on event registrations
  • Payment processing fees (typically 1.9% + ¬£0.20) passed through
‚ÑπÔ∏è
Note: You'll only be charged a commission on completed bookings. Cancelled reservations and no-shows do not incur commission fees.

Setting Prices

Effectively price your tables and events:

  • Research local market rates for similar gaming spaces
  • Consider variable pricing based on table type and features
  • Implement peak and off-peak pricing to maximize occupancy
  • Create special rates for regular customers or longer bookings

Payment Reports

Access detailed financial information:

  • View transaction history in the Reports section
  • Filter by date range, table, or payment status
  • Export reports for accounting purposes
  • Track commissions and payment processing fees

Handling Refunds

Process refunds when necessary:

  • Full refunds can be processed up to 7 days after the reservation
  • Partial refunds are available based on your cancellation policy
  • Issue store credit as an alternative to refunds
  • Refunded reservations will not incur commission fees
